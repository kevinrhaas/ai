Upgrade CE to EE
1. Description of the addressable and serviceable market by industry, personas (user, buyer)
- Addressable Market: All industries with a need for data integration and analytics, especially those with large and complex data environments.
- Serviceable Market: Mid to large size companies that have outgrown the Community Edition (CE) and need the advanced features of Enterprise Edition (EE).
- User Personas: Data engineers, data architects, IT managers, business analysts, and data scientists.
- Buyer Personas: CIOs, CTOs, and IT directors.

2. The customer problem being solved?
- Customer Problem: 
  - Inability to handle the volume and complexity of data with the limited features of CE.
  - Lack of scalability and flexibility in data integration and analytics.
  - Inability to meet regulatory compliance requirements.
  - Inability to integrate with other enterprise systems and applications.
- Sample Use Case: A financial services company that needs to integrate data from multiple sources, including legacy systems, to comply with regulatory requirements.

3. What's business outcome does the customer need to achieve?
- Business Outcome: 
  - Improved data quality, faster time to insights, and increased revenue and profitability.
  - Compliance with regulatory requirements.
  - Scalability and flexibility in data integration and analytics.
  - Integration with other enterprise systems and applications.
- Sample Use Case: The financial services company can quickly generate regulatory reports and reduce the risk of non-compliance.

4. Why is the customer trying to solve this? What does success look like?
- Customer Motivation: To improve business agility and competitiveness by leveraging data as a strategic asset.
- Success Criteria: Improved data quality, faster time to insights, and increased revenue and profitability.

5. How else can the customer solve this problem?
- Alternative Solutions: Custom-built data integration and analytics solutions or point solutions from multiple vendors.
- Disadvantages: Higher costs, longer implementation times, and increased complexity.

6. How are competitors solving this for their customers?
- Competitor Solutions: Similar data integration and analytics solutions, but with varying levels of functionality and scalability.
- Differentiators: Pentaho's open-source architecture, flexible deployment options, and seamless integration with Hitachi's Lumada platform.

7. Why are they unable to resolve this with the technology they have today?
- Technology Limitations: The limitations of CE and other point solutions that are unable to handle the volume and variety of data or lack the necessary functionality to integrate and transform data across multiple sources.

8. What is our offering? Is it platform, technology capabilities, product or solution?
- Offering: Upgrade from CE to EE, which includes advanced features for data integration, data quality, and business analytics, and integration with Hitachi's Lumada platform.
- Differentiators: Open-source architecture, flexibility, and scalability.

9. What is the solution design (or solution blueprint) into which our product fits?
- Solution Blueprint: An end-to-end data management and analytics solution that includes data integration, data quality, data cataloging, and business analytics.
- The solution design includes:
  - Data governance policies and procedures
  - Automated data management and protection tools
  - Lumada Data Catalog and analytics capabilities
  - Integration with existing IT infrastructure
- Sample Use Case: The financial services company can integrate data from multiple sources and generate regulatory reports in a timely and efficient manner.

  - First contact: Identify potential customers and schedule a meeting to understand their needs.
